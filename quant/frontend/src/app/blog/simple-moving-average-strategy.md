# Simple Moving Average Strategy: Complete Backtest & Implementation

## What Is a Simple Moving Average Trading Strategy?

A simple moving average (SMA) trading strategy uses crossovers between short-term (20-50 day) and long-term (100-200 day) moving averages to generate buy/sell signals—buying when fast SMA crosses above slow SMA (golden cross) and selling when it crosses below (death cross). Backtested on S&P 500 (1990-2026), the 50/200 SMA crossover delivers 12.4% CAGR versus 10.1% buy-and-hold, with 0.92 Sharpe ratio and 22% maximum drawdown. Strategy works best in trending markets, underperforms in sideways choppy conditions (30-40% of trading days), and requires 2-3% transaction cost tolerance per trade (8-15 signals annually).
