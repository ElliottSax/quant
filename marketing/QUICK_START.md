## Day 1: Get Oriented

1. Read `README.md` (this directory's overview)
2. Skim `01-landing-page.md` (understand core messaging)
3. Review `04-social-proof.md` (see what users are saying)

**Time:** 20 minutes

---

## Day 2: Customize Core Materials

1. **Update all statistics** with your actual numbers:
   - User count
   - Performance metrics (return %, accuracy %)
   - Feature list
   - Testimonials/case studies

2. **Replace placeholders:**
   - [Founder Name]
   - [Company Name]
   - [Contact email]
   - [Your website]

3. **Verify claims:**
   - All statistics should be from your actual data
   - Backtesting results accurate
   - User testimonials real quotes

**Time:** 2-3 hours

---

## Day 3: Set Up Infrastructure

1. **Email platform** - Set up nurture sequence in Mailchimp/ConvertKit/etc
2. **Landing page** - Copy sections into your web platform
3. **Blog platform** - Set up for publishing (WordPress, Medium, Substack)
4. **Social media** - Schedule buffer, plan posting calendar
5. **Press distribution** - Create media list, prepare PR software

**Time:** 1-2 hours

---

## Day 4: Prepare for Launch

1. **Schedule blog posts** - Plan 2-3 per week for next month
2. **Write emails** - Input into email platform with timing
3. **Create graphics** - Make visual versions of landing page copy
4. **Test everything** - Verify links, forms, signup flows
5. **Brief team** - Make sure everyone knows messaging

**Time:** 2-3 hours

## FAQ

### Q: Can I copy this content directly?
A: Yes, but customize it with your specific data, metrics, and testimonials. Don't just copy-paste without changing numbers.

### Q: Which files are most important?
A: In order: (1) Landing page, (2) Email sequence, (3) Blog posts, (4) Social proof, (5) Everything else.

### Q: What if I don't have all the statistics yet?
A: Use ranges ("up to 50%"), add "projected" where needed, or focus on what you do have. Transparency is more important than perfect numbers.

### Q: How do I adapt this for my audience?
A: Read each section's "SEO keywords" and examples. Tailor the language and examples to your specific audience (traders vs. advisors vs. funds).

### Q: Can I translate this to other languages?
A: Yes. The content is well-structured and can be translated. Keep key terms (like "congressional trades") consistent.

### Q: How often should I update this content?
A: Update statistics monthly, add new testimonials/case studies as they come in, refresh blog topics quarterly.
