## The Challenge: Data vs. Signal

Every day, 100+ congressional trades are filed. Most are noise. A few are signal.

The difference between traders who profit from congressional data and those who don't comes down to one skill: **reading the signals that matter.**

This guide teaches you exactly what to look for.

---

## The Three Questions You Should Ask

When you see a congressional trade, ask:

1. **Is this a conviction trade?** (Is the politician serious or just rebalancing?)
2. **What does their position suggest they know?** (What information do they have access to?)
3. **When are others making similar moves?** (Is this isolated or coordinated?)

Let's break down each.

---

## Part 1: Conviction Signals

### What Makes a Trade Worth Paying Attention To?

Not all trades are equal. Here's how to separate signal from noise:

#### Signal #1: Significant Position Increase
**What to look for:** A politician who owns a stock and increases their position by 50%+ in a short timeframe (6 months or less).

**Why it matters:**
- Small purchases could be automatic 401k contributions
- Large increases show deliberate choice
- Sizing reveals confidence level

**Example:** A representative owns 500 shares of Apple. They buy 350 more shares within 3 months. That's a 70% increase. Conviction signal.

**Red flag:** Someone who owns 100 shares and buys another 150 shares might just be rebalancing monthly. Look for substantial increases relative to existing position size.

#### Signal #2: New Position Entry
**What to look for:** A politician buying a stock they don't currently own.

**Why it matters:**
- New entries are often research-driven
- They suggest discovery of something new, not routine investing
- Requires deliberate decision

**Example:** Senator doesn't own any Tesla. They buy 1,000 shares for $200k. This is a fresh conviction, not rebalancing.

**How to weigh it:** New entries matter more than adding to existing positions, but the position sizing still matters:
- Buying 100 shares of a $1,000 stock = probably a small bet
- Buying 1,000 shares = serious conviction

#### Signal #3: Increase During Weakness
**What to look for:** A politician buying a stock that's down 20%+ from recent highs.

**Why it matters:**
- Buying strength is different from buying dips
- Buying weakness shows contrarian conviction
- Suggests they know something about the bottom

**Example:** Nvidia down from $880 to $620 (-30%) in November 2024. Politician increases position 100%. They're buying the dip because they believe in recovery.

**Contrast:** Buying a stock at all-time highs after a 40% run suggests different conviction than catching it on the way down.

#### Signal #4: Focused Sector Rotation
**What to look for:** A politician reducing one sector while increasing another.

**Why it matters:**
- Sector rotation is intentional
- Suggests view on macro trends
- Different from simple buying/selling

**Example:**
- Sells 50% of energy position
- Buys 100% more in technology
- Sells all healthcare
This suggests shift in market outlook.

### Conviction Score Framework

Rate congressional trades on this scale:

```
CONVICTION SCORE:
1/5: Small buy/sell, existing position, routine rebalancing
2/5: Modest increase (20-30%), new position, but small size
3/5: Clear increase (50%+), moderate size, or new position with size
4/5: Major increase (100%+), significant capital deployment
5/5: Complete sector rotation, large new positions, buying into weakness
```

**Rule:** Only pay attention to 4/5 and 5/5 trades. The others are mostly noise.

## Part 2: What Their Position Reveals

### Reading the Information Signal

Members of Congress have access to non-public information through their committees. The best traders learn to "read" what politicians know based on:
1. Which committee they're on
2. What stocks they're buying
3. When the buys happen relative to their committee calendar

#### The Committee Map

Different committees provide different information:

**Banking & Financial Services Committee**
- Access to: Interest rate decisions, Fed policy, banking regulations
- Watch for: Trades in financial institutions, banks, payment processors
- Timing clue: Trading around Fed meeting dates, banking regulation announcements

**Energy & Natural Resources Committee**
- Access to: Energy policy, infrastructure spending, renewable regulations
- Watch for: Oil, gas, utilities, renewable energy companies
- Timing clue: Trading before energy legislation votes, around policy announcements

**Healthcare Committee**
- Access to: FDA approvals, healthcare legislation, drug policy
- Watch for: Pharma companies, healthcare providers, biotech
- Timing clue: Trading before approval announcements, healthcare bill votes

**Armed Services Committee**
- Access to: Defense spending, military contractor performance, geopolitical decisions
- Watch for: Defense contractors, aerospace, cybersecurity
- Timing clue: Trading before defense budget announcements, military operations

**Ways & Means Committee (House) / Finance Committee (Senate)**
- Access to: Tax policy, revenue decisions, economic outlook
- Watch for: Everything—these are the broadest committees
- Timing clue: Trading before tax legislation, around economic reports

**Example Reading:**
Senator on Banking Committee increases position in regional bank stocks → Likely learned about favorable regulatory changes or rate decisions coming.

Representative on Healthcare adds biotech position → Likely knows about pending FDA approvals in that sector.

#### The Timing Signal

Congressional trades cluster around committee calendars. Smart traders watch for:

**Pre-announcement buying:**
- Committee members buy before their committee votes on a bill
- This reveals expectations about the vote outcome
- Trading 1-2 weeks before announcement often predicts the direction

**Earnings-correlated timing:**
- Politicians on relevant committees often increase positions 1-2 weeks before earnings
- This suggests knowledge of pending results
- Check if trade timing precedes earnings by 1-3 weeks

**Macro timing:**
- Trades around Fed announcements by Banking Committee members
- Trades around inflation reports by Finance Committee members
- This suggests knowledge of outcomes before official release

## Part 3: Coordinated Trading Signals

### The Power of Group Behavior

Individual trades are useful. Coordinated trades are powerful.

When multiple politicians trade the same stock at the same time, that's a signal worth taking seriously.

#### What Coordination Looks Like

**Type 1: Same Committee, Same Stock**
- 4 members of Healthcare Committee all buy Pfizer within 3 weeks
- They likely shared information in committee meeting
- Suggests consensus about pharma outlook

**Type 2: Both Parties, Same Stock**
- Republican senator and Democratic representative both buy Apple
- Bipartisan agreement is rare and meaningful
- Suggests non-partisan information (strong fundamentals, not political views)

**Type 3: Sector Rotation Cluster**
- 8 politicians across committees reduce energy positions
- 6 of same politicians increase tech positions
- Suggests macro view shift (rotation from value to growth, etc.)

**Type 4: New Position Cluster**
- 5 politicians all enter same stock within 2 weeks
- All are new to the position (not adding to existing)
- Suggests discovery event or major news

#### Coordination Analysis Framework

```
How to read coordination strength:

WEAK signal:
- 2 politicians, different parties, different committees, different positions
- Timing: 6+ weeks apart
- Position size: Small (< $100k per politician)

MODERATE signal:
- 4-6 politicians, same or related committees
- Timing: 2-4 weeks apart
- Position size: Medium ($100k-$500k per politician)
- Same direction (all buying, all selling)

STRONG signal:
- 8+ politicians, same party or same committee
- Timing: Within 1 week of each other
- Position size: Large ($500k+)
- Coordinated action (buying weakness, selling strength, etc.)

VERY STRONG signal:
- 10+ politicians across multiple committees
- Both parties participating (bipartisan)
- Timing: Tightly clustered (within days)
- Position size: Very large, percentage of their portfolio
- Action: Major position building or complete sector exit
```

**Example of Strong Signal:**
- September 2023: 12 Banking Committee members increase bank stock positions within 10 days
- Coordinated with Fed signals about rate outlook
- Banks outperformed S&P by 800bps over next 6 months

## Part 4: Red Flags & Noise Filters

### What to IGNORE

Not all congressional trades are worth attention. Filter out noise by ignoring:

#### Red Flag 1: Inherited Positions
When politicians inherit positions through estate plans, they often don't represent conviction.

**How to spot:** Look for notes in filings indicating inheritance or spousal transfers.

**Why it's noise:** Inherited position increase doesn't reflect the politician's view—it's just estate administration.

#### Red Flag 2: Tax Loss Harvesting
Politicians also practice tax-loss harvesting to offset gains.

**How to spot:** Look for sell patterns:
- Selling at loss (stock down from purchase price)
- Often paired with buying similar stock in another fund
- Occurs in November-December (tax year end)

**Why it's noise:** Tax strategy, not market conviction.

#### Red Flag 3: Automatic Rebalancing
Many politicians use automatic portfolio rebalancing (e.g., "buy when position drops below 5% of portfolio").

**How to spot:**
- Regular, predictable purchase amounts
- Same dollar amount year after year
- Consistent timing (quarterly or semi-annual)

**Why it's noise:** Mechanical, not conviction-driven.

#### Red Flag 4: Spousal or Family Trades
When trades involve spouse or dependent children accounts, they may not reflect the politician's direct knowledge.

**How to spot:** Filing will note family member's account.

**Why it matters:** Family member may have different information access or risk tolerance.

#### Red Flag 5: Small Trades
Position changes under $25,000 are often noise.

**How to spot:** Low dollar amount relative to net worth.

**Why it's noise:** Too small to represent real allocation decision for wealthy politicians.

#### Red Flag 6: Recent Wash Sales
If a politician sold a position 3 months ago at a loss, then buys it back, the buyback is less meaningful.

**How to spot:** Check 45-90 day trading history on same stock.

**Why it matters:** They may be reversing a mistake, not making a new conviction.

## Part 5: Practical Trading Framework

### A System for Using Congressional Data

Here's how professional traders use this information:

#### Step 1: Daily Scan
- Check Quant's new trades daily
- Filter for conviction trades only (4/5 or 5/5 conviction score)
- Identify committees involved
- Note any coordination with other traders

#### Step 2: Context Research
- What's happening in that stock/sector?
- What relevant news has been published?
- What earnings dates are coming?
- What are the technical chart levels?

#### Step 3: Compare to Your Views
- Does the congressional trade align with your research?
- Are you adding to existing conviction or starting fresh?
- What's your thesis and how does it change based on insider trade?

#### Step 4: Position Sizing
- Congressional trades are signals, not buy signals
- Position size proportionally to your conviction
- Diversify across multiple signals
- Never over-concentrate based on any single trade

#### Step 5: Risk Management
- Set stop losses like any other trade
- Don't hold positions past major news events they're tied to
- Monitor position against technical support/resistance
- Track whether coordination thesis continues to play out

#### Step 6: Performance Tracking
- Track how congressional-inspired trades perform vs. your baseline strategy
- A/B test congressional signals vs. your existing system
- Measure: Win rate, average return per trade, correlation to market
- Iterate and improve your signal recognition

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Chasing every trade** - Only 10-15% of congressional trades are actionable signals. Filter ruthlessly.

2. **Ignoring context** - A trade means nothing without understanding the politician's committee, company fundamentals, and market context.

3. **Expecting exact timing** - Politicians might buy before 6-month move, but market moves within weeks. Have patience.

4. **Over-concentrating** - No single signal is 100% right. Diversify your conviction.

5. **Not setting stop losses** - Congressional traders are right more often than average, but they're not right every time. Protect yourself.

6. **Ignoring contradictions** - If politician sells while committee pushes bullish policy, something's off. Investigate.

7. **Missing the lag** - Sometimes markets price information faster than politicians trade. Look for where capital is already flowing.

## The Bottom Line

Reading congressional trades like a pro means:

1. **Distinguish signal from noise** - Only conviction trades matter
2. **Understand information access** - What does their committee tell them?
3. **Recognize coordination** - When politicians trade together, it's stronger
4. **Filter out red flags** - Tax trades, inherited positions, and small positions are noise
5. **Apply disciplined trading** - Use congressional data as one signal, not the only signal
6. **Track and iterate** - Measure what works and improve your process

The politicians who trade best aren't doing anything illegal. They're simply using the legitimate information access their position provides. By learning to read those same signals, you can benefit from the same informational advantage.
