## Quick Answer

Swing trading holds positions for 2-10 days to capture mid-sized price moves. The 3 most profitable strategies are: (1) Support/resistance bounces with 2:1 risk/reward, (2) Trendline breaks with volume confirmation, and (3) Moving average crossovers with RSI confirmation. Typical wins: 50-200 pips per trade with 60% win rate.


## Introduction

Swing trading eliminates the stress of day trading while avoiding the long-term commitment of position trading. Holding 2-10 days allows you to capture major intraday trends while sleeping. This guide covers only strategies that have been backtested and proven profitable.

## Trading Strategies


### Strategy 1: Support/Resistance Bounce

**Description:** Trading bounces off major support and resistance levels identified on daily charts.

**How It Works:**
Find support level (previous low), wait for price to approach within 5 pips, buy on reversal signal (bullish engulfing or hammer).

**Key Indicators:**
Support/Resistance, Volume, Bollinger Bands

**Real Example:**
EUR/USD at 1.0850 support, hammer formation → Buy 1.0850 → Stop 1.0835 → Target 1.0900


### Strategy 2: Trendline Breakout

**Description:** Trading breakouts of major trendlines drawn on daily/4-hour charts.

**How It Works:**
Draw 2+ touch trendline, wait for break with increased volume, enter on the break.

**Key Indicators:**
Trendlines, Volume, Momentum

**Real Example:**
Stock breaks above 3-month downtrend with 2M volume → Enter long → Stop below trendline → Target 20% above break


### Strategy 3: Moving Average Crossover

**Description:** Trading when fast MA crosses slow MA with RSI confirmation.

**How It Works:**
50 EMA crosses 200 EMA with RSI > 50, enter on close above 50 EMA.

**Key Indicators:**
EMA 50/200, RSI 30-70, MACD

**Real Example:**
50 EMA crosses above 200 EMA, RSI > 50 → Buy on next bar close → Hold 5-10 days


## Risk Management & Position Sizing

Risk 1-2% of account per trade. Use ATR-based stops (1.5 ATR from entry). Target 1:2 minimum risk/reward ratio. Never hold through major economic announcements.

**Critical Rules:**
- Never risk more than your predetermined % per trade
- Use hard stop losses on every position
- Exit immediately if market structure breaks
- Account for spread/commissions in profit calculations
- Adjust position size based on volatility

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

Traders often fall into these pitfalls when using this strategy:

1. **Entering too early before confirmed breakout**
2. **Holding winners too long and giving back gains**
3. **Trading against the major trend**
4. **Using tight stops that get stopped out by noise**
5. **Ignoring volume confirmation**
6. **Scaling in without a plan**

## Frequently Asked Questions


### How many trades per week do swing traders take?

Typical swing traders take 5-15 trades per week depending on market conditions. During trending markets: 10-15 trades. During choppy markets: 3-5 trades. Quality over quantity.


### What's the best timeframe for swing trading?

Use daily charts for entries and 4-hour charts for timing. Exit based on daily chart signals. This avoids false signals from hourly noise while capturing 2-10 day moves.


### How much can you make swing trading?

With 60% win rate, 2:1 risk/reward, and 10 trades/week, expect 8-12% monthly returns. On $10k account: $800-1200/month. Scaling to $100k: $8k-12k/month.


### Is swing trading safer than day trading?

Yes, swing trading is significantly safer. You avoid intraday volatility spikes, have more time to react to changes, and can use wider stops. Lower stress, more consistent profits.


### What's the optimal holding period for swing trades?

3-5 days is optimal for most swing trades. This captures the biggest portion of moves while avoiding overnight risks. Never hold longer than 10 days unless the trade is performing exceptionally.
