## Quick Answer

Trend following buys assets making new highs with momentum and holds for weeks/months. Rules: (1) Enter on MA crossover (50 > 200 EMA) or breakout of resistance, (2) Exit when 50 < 200 EMA or price breaks below support, (3) Hold through retracements. Win rate: 40-50%, but winners 2-5x larger than losers. Annual returns: 20-50% with proper risk management.


## Introduction

Trend following is the most profitable long-term strategy. It removes the guesswork of market timing and lets you capture the biggest moves. This guide teaches the professional approach.

## Trading Strategies


### Strategy 1: Moving Average Trend Following

**Description:** Buy when price breaks above 200-day MA, hold while price stays above MA, exit on breakdown.

**How It Works:**
Daily chart: price breaks above 200 EMA + 50 EMA above 200 EMA. Hold long as this remains true. Exit when price closes below 50 EMA.

**Key Indicators:**
EMA 50/200, ADX > 25, Support/Resistance

**Real Example:**
SPY at $450, 50 EMA ($445) above 200 EMA ($440). Buy $450. Hold through $455-460 resistance. Target: $475+ over next 2-3 weeks.


### Strategy 2: Higher High/Higher Low Trend Following

**Description:** Buy on breakout of previous resistance when higher lows and higher highs are forming.

**How It Works:**
In uptrend: each swing high is higher, each swing low is higher. Buy at each new resistance breakout. Hold until lower low breaks (downtrend confirmation).

**Key Indicators:**
Higher highs/lows, Volume, RSI > 50

**Real Example:**
Stock: $90 high, $85 low, $95 high, $87 low (trending up). Buy at $95. Hold to $100+. Stop: if breaks $87.


### Strategy 3: Breakout Trend Following

**Description:** Buy breakouts of major resistance levels (6-month highs, all-time highs).

**How It Works:**
Stock trading near all-time high of $150. Breaks above $150 on volume. Enter. Hold for the trend: likely to $160-180+ over 8-12 weeks.

**Key Indicators:**
Resistance breakout, Volume spike, Momentum

**Real Example:**
NVDA breaks $120 all-time high on earnings. Buy $121. Hold through retracements. Target: $140-150 over 3 months.


## Risk Management & Position Sizing

Risk 1-2% per trade but with larger position sizes for trending stocks. Use 5-7% trailing stops (allow room for volatility). Exit on technical breakdown (MA cross or support breach).

**Critical Rules:**
- Never risk more than your predetermined % per trade
- Use hard stop losses on every position
- Exit immediately if market structure breaks
- Account for spread/commissions in profit calculations
- Adjust position size based on volatility

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

Traders often fall into these pitfalls when using this strategy:

1. **Exiting too early (missing 50%+ of the move)**
2. **Using tight stops that get stopped out by retracements**
3. **Entering late in trends (last 20% of move, first 20% down)**
4. **Trend trading against the weekly/monthly trend**
5. **Not confirming trends with volume**
6. **Averaging down in losing positions**

## Frequently Asked Questions


### What's the difference between trend following and momentum trading?

Trend: hold weeks/months, big 20-100% moves. Momentum: hold days, quick 10-20% moves. Trend: allows retracements. Momentum: exits on slowdown. Both profitable, different timeframes.


### How long do typical trends last?

Stock trends: 2-12 weeks typical. Crypto trends: 4-16 weeks. Forex trends: 4-20 weeks. Longer trends: rarer but possible (3-6 month trends captured 50%+ moves).


### Can you make money in downtrends?

Yes, short the same way: 200 EMA below 50 EMA = downtrend. Short resistance breakdowns. Same rules apply in reverse. Down trends often faster/sharper than uptrends.


### What's the best time period for moving averages?

Stock trends: 50/200 day MA. Faster trends: 20/50 MA. Crypto trends: 50/200 weekly MA. Use longer MA for longer-term trends.


### How much can trend followers make annually?

Conservative: 20-30% annually. Moderate: 30-50% annually. Aggressive: 50-100%+ annually. Key: hit big trends 3-4 times per year. Most losses from choppy markets between trends.
