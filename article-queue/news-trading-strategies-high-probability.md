## Quick Answer

News trading profits from price spikes around earnings/economic data. Top strategies: (1) Sell into strength on Fed announcement reversals ($500-2000 per trade), (2) Straddle earnings: buy calls + puts for any direction move (50-100% on $500 investment), (3) Buy oversold bounces after negative news (70% win rate). Key: trade within 60 seconds of news release.


## Introduction

News moves create 2-5% intraday price swaps in seconds. This guide teaches how to trade safely around news without getting blown out of positions.

## Trading Strategies


### Strategy 1: News Reversal Trading

**Description:** Trade the reversal after initial news reaction when price has moved too far.

**How It Works:**
Bad earnings announcement → Stock drops 5%. Trading extremes: if RSI < 20, buy the bounce. Initial move often overshoots; reversals are fast and profitable.

**Key Indicators:**
RSI extremes < 20 or > 80, Volume profile, Support/Resistance

**Real Example:**
TSLA misses earnings → drops to $200 → RSI 15 (oversold) → Buy at $199 → Bounces to $205 in minutes → $600 profit


### Strategy 2: Earnings Straddle

**Description:** Buy out-of-the-money calls and puts to profit from any large move (up or down).

**How It Works:**
Company earnings: $500 call premium + $500 put premium = $1000 risk. Stock moves 5%+ either direction: one side profits $1500+.

**Key Indicators:**
Implied volatility, Historical move size, Support/Resistance

**Real Example:**
Apple earnings: buy 5 calls at $180 strike, 5 puts at $170 strike for $1000 total. Apple drops to $165 or up to $185: straddle nets $1500+ profit.


### Strategy 3: Fed Announcement Scalping

**Description:** Trade the spike and reversal within the first 2 minutes of Fed/ECB announcement.

**How It Works:**
Fed announces rate hold. Initial spike reaction in first 30 seconds. Reversal happens next 60-90 seconds. Sell spike, cover quickly for fast profits.

**Key Indicators:**
Sentiment (hawkish/dovish), Initial spike direction, Volume

**Real Example:**
Fed holds rates → USD spikes 1% in 20 seconds → Sell USD at spike → Buy back 2 minutes later at lower price → $300 profit per lot


## Risk Management & Position Sizing

Never hold overnight through earnings. Use 3-5% wider stops (volatility increases). Trade within 2 minutes of news release (slippage after). Size down 50% (news trades are riskier).

**Critical Rules:**
- Never risk more than your predetermined % per trade
- Use hard stop losses on every position
- Exit immediately if market structure breaks
- Account for spread/commissions in profit calculations
- Adjust position size based on volatility

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

Traders often fall into these pitfalls when using this strategy:

1. **Trading news in low volume sessions (slippage kills profits)**
2. **Not knowing which economic data points matter most**
3. **Holding through entire earnings call (volatility drops after initial move)**
4. **Using small accounts on news (commissions/spreads eat profits)**
5. **Trading surprised direction (news-driven reversals, not expected moves)**
6. **No stop losses on news trades (one bad trade = month of losses)**

## Frequently Asked Questions


### Which news events move markets most?

Major: Fed decisions, employment data (NFP), GDP, CPI. Company: earnings surprises (beat/miss). Crypto: Bitcoin ETF approvals, regulation. Check economic calendar: Tradingeconomics.com


### How long do news-driven moves last?

Initial spike: 30-60 seconds. Reversal phase: next 2-5 minutes. Extended moves: 30+ minutes (if news is truly shocking). Best trading: first 5 minutes.


### Can you make money news trading beginners?

Yes, but risky. Requires: perfect timing, small positions, hard stops, fast exits. Start with earnings straddles (lower risk/reward) vs. scalping (higher risk/reward).
