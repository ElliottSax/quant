## Quick Answer

Range traders identify horizontal support/resistance and trade the bounces. Setup: buy 2-5 pips above support, sell 2-5 pips below resistance. Target: 50-70% of range height. Optimal: 3-5 bounces daily per range = $500-1000 daily profit on $10k account.


## Introduction

Markets spend 80% of time in ranges. Range trading captures all the daily profits from these sideways moves. This guide shows how to identify and trade them.

## Trading Strategies


### Strategy 1: Classic Support/Resistance Bounce

**Description:** Buy when price approaches support, sell when it approaches resistance.

**How It Works:**
Identify 2-3 day support level and 2-3 day resistance level. Buy 2-5 pips above support. Sell 2-5 pips below resistance.

**Key Indicators:**
Support/Resistance levels, RSI 30-70, Volume at levels

**Real Example:**
EUR/USD ranging 1.0800-1.0850. Buy at 1.0802, sell at 1.0848. Repeat daily 3-5 times: 45 pips profit per cycle × 3 = 135 pips daily.


### Strategy 2: Fibonacci Range Trading

**Description:** Use Fibonacci levels within the range to identify bounce points.

**How It Works:**
Range: 1.0800-1.0850. Fib levels: 23.6% (1.0812), 50% (1.0825), 78.6% (1.0838). Trade bounces off Fib levels.

**Key Indicators:**
Fibonacci levels, Volume, RSI

**Real Example:**
Price drops to 50% Fib level (1.0825) → Buy → Bounce to 23.6% level (1.0838) → Sell


### Strategy 3: Bollinger Bands Range Trading

**Description:** Trade when price touches Bollinger Bands upper/lower in ranging market.

**How It Works:**
Tight Bollinger Bands = ranging (good). Price near upper band = sell. Price near lower band = buy. Target: middle band.

**Key Indicators:**
Bollinger Bands, RSI, Volume

**Real Example:**
QQQ upper BB = $360, lower BB = $340, middle = $350. Buy at $340.50, target $349.50. Sell at $359.50, target $350.50.


## Risk Management & Position Sizing

Risk 1-2% per range trade. Use tight stops: 3-5 pips (forex) or 0.1-0.3% (stocks). Exit at resistance/support, don't hold beyond range. Multiple small trades > holding one big trade.

**Critical Rules:**
- Never risk more than your predetermined % per trade
- Use hard stop losses on every position
- Exit immediately if market structure breaks
- Account for spread/commissions in profit calculations
- Adjust position size based on volatility

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

Traders often fall into these pitfalls when using this strategy:

1. **Range trading in trending markets (breakouts destroy ranges)**
2. **Support/resistance levels identified incorrectly (too wide)**
3. **Wide stops that exceed the range (guarantees losses)**
4. **Holding positions at resistance/support (breakouts happen suddenly)**
5. **Not accounting for spreads/commissions in tight ranges**
6. **Trading low-liquidity assets with wide bid-ask**

## Frequently Asked Questions


### How do you identify if a market is actually ranging?

ADX < 20-25 (weak trend). Bollinger Bands tightening (low volatility). Price bouncing 3+ times between levels. No new highs/lows for 3+ days.


### What's the minimum range width to trade profitably?

Minimum: range must be 2x your stop loss size. Example: 10 pip stop = 20+ pip range minimum. Smaller ranges: tight stops, small profits, more commissions eat returns.


### How many trades should you take in a range?

Typical: 3-8 trades per day in a good range. Quality: 3-5 perfect setups > 10 mediocre ones. Stop trading when range breaks (new high/low).


### What happens when the range breaks?

Stop range trading immediately. Switch to breakout/trend trading. Close partial positions at range edge. Never hold through breakouts in range trades.


### Which assets are best for range trading?

Forex: best (clear ranges, tight spreads). Stocks: good (ranges during consolidation). Crypto: okay (high volatility breaks ranges). Avoid: trending markets.
