## Quick Answer

Position trading holds for 2-12 weeks capturing major trend moves (20-100%+). Strategy: (1) Buy on MA crossovers (50 > 200 EMA bullish), (2) Hold through retracements, (3) Exit when 50 EMA crosses back below 200 EMA. Wide stops: 5-10% below entry. Typical hold: 4-8 weeks. Target: 15-50% per trade.


## Introduction

Position trading combines the best of trend following with the patience of long-term investing. By holding 4-12 weeks, you capture the largest profitable moves with the least stress. This guide covers position trading fundamentals.

## Trading Strategies


### Strategy 1: Moving Average Crossover Trend

**Description:** Buying when 50 EMA crosses above 200 EMA and holding until the reverse happens.

**How It Works:**
Daily chart: 50 EMA above 200 EMA = uptrend. Hold as long as this remains true. Exit when 50 crosses back below 200.

**Key Indicators:**
EMA 50/200, ADX > 25, Support/Resistance

**Real Example:**
QQQ 50 EMA > 200 EMA (uptrend) → Buy close → Hold 4-12 weeks → Exit when 50 < 200 EMA


### Strategy 2: Breakout of Major Resistance

**Description:** Buying the breakout of major resistance levels (6-12 month highs).

**How It Works:**
Stock near all-time high, breaks above resistance on strong volume. Hold until 50 EMA breaks below.

**Key Indicators:**
Resistance level, Volume, RSI > 50

**Real Example:**
TSLA breaks $250 all-time high → Buy → Hold until MA breakdown or 50-100% gain target


### Strategy 3: Fundamental Trend Trading

**Description:** Buying stocks entering new revenue cycles or recovery from lows.

**How It Works:**
Company releases positive earnings, stock breaks above consolidation. Hold through the recovery cycle (typically 4-12 weeks).

**Key Indicators:**
Earnings growth, Revenue growth, Technical breakout

**Real Example:**
Tech company beat earnings, breaks $100 → Buy → Hold 8 weeks for 30-50% move → Exit after peak


## Risk Management & Position Sizing

Risk 2-3% per position (wider stops than day trading). Use 5-7% stops below key support. Position size based on volatility (ATR). Never average down, instead take losses quickly.

**Critical Rules:**
- Never risk more than your predetermined % per trade
- Use hard stop losses on every position
- Exit immediately if market structure breaks
- Account for spread/commissions in profit calculations
- Adjust position size based on volatility

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

Traders often fall into these pitfalls when using this strategy:

1. **Exiting winning positions too early**
2. **Averaging down in losing positions**
3. **Not having clear exit rules (hold forever mentality)**
4. **Using tight stops that get stopped out by noise**
5. **Trading without support/resistance levels**
6. **Holding through earnings (too risky for position trades)**

## Frequently Asked Questions


### How much can position traders make annually?

Conservative: 20-30% annually. Moderate: 30-50% annually. Aggressive: 50-100%+ annually. Depends on trend quality and risk management. Starting with $10k: realistic target $2k-3k profit annually.


### Should position traders worry about daily volatility?

No, that's the advantage. Daily 5-10% swings are noise. Position trades benefit from wide stops. Only exit on technical breakdown (MA cross) or fundamental change.


### How many positions should you hold at once?

Typically 3-5 positions for diversification. Never more than 10 unless very small positions. Concentration: higher returns but higher risk. 3-5 = optimal balance.


### What's the best timeframe to identify position trade setups?

Daily chart for entry/exit signals, weekly chart for trend confirmation. Never trade against the weekly trend. Entry on daily signal, confirmation from weekly.


### Can position traders make money in bear markets?

Yes, trade the downtrend. Short 50 EMA when it crosses below 200 EMA. Hold short until 50 > 200 again. Same principles apply in reverse.
