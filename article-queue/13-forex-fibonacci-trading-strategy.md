## Risk Management: Protecting Your Trading Capital

Risk management is the cornerstone of long-term profitability in forex trading. Without proper risk controls, even the best strategies fail eventually.

### Position Sizing Rules

**Calculate correct position size using this formula:**

Position Size (in lots) = (Account Size × Risk Percentage) / (Pips at Risk × Pip Value)

**Example calculation:**
- Account: $10,000
- Risk per trade: 2% ($200)
- Stop loss distance: 20 pips
- EUR/USD pip value: $10 per pip
- Position size = $200 / (20 × $10) = 1.0 lot

### Risk/Reward Ratio Management

Professional traders never risk $1 to make $1. Minimum acceptable ratios:

- **1:2 ratio**: Risk 20 pips to make 40 pips (acceptable minimum)
- **1:3 ratio**: Risk 20 pips to make 60 pips (preferred for swing trades)
- **1:4 ratio**: Risk 20 pips to make 80 pips (ideal for position trades)

Only trade setups offering at least 1:2 risk/reward. This ensures that even with 50% win rate, you achieve profitability. For example, winning 5 trades of 20 pips and losing 5 trades of 40 pips = break-even. This math is why risk/reward matters more than win rate.

### Portfolio Risk Management

- **Maximum daily loss**: Stop trading after losing 2% of account in single day
- **Maximum weekly loss**: Pause strategy if weekly losses exceed 5% of account
- **Per-trade risk**: Never risk more than 1-2% of account on single trade
- **Correlation risk**: Limit exposure to correlated pairs (avoid 5 EUR crosses simultaneously)

**Capital preservation is priority #1.** Large losses are much harder to recover from than many realize. A 50% account loss requires 100% gain to break-even. A 20% loss requires only 25% gain to recover. This asymmetry is why protecting capital matters more than chasing profits.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

Professional traders who mastered the fibonacci retracements warn about these critical mistakes:

### Mistake #1: Trading Partial Signals
**Error**: Entering trades when only 2 out of 3 entry criteria are met, hoping the third will align.
**Impact**: 40-50% of trades fail immediately, killing overall profitability.
**Fix**: Wait for ALL entry criteria. Patience is the trader's best tool. Missing one trade costs far less than losing money on a bad trade.

### Mistake #2: Moving Stop Losses Incorrectly
**Error**: Moving stops to breakeven or widening stops after a small loss.
**Impact**: Turns minor losses into catastrophic ones.
**Fix**: Stop-loss placement is final. If the setup is no longer valid, the trade should be closed, not adjusted.

### Mistake #3: Over-leveraging Position Size
**Error**: Trading 5-10% account risk per trade instead of 1-2%.
**Impact**: One bad losing streak destroys the account.
**Fix**: Calculate precise position size based on stop-loss distance and account size. Discipline here prevents 90% of blow-ups.

### Mistake #4: Ignoring Risk/Reward Ratios
**Error**: Closing profitable trades at 15 pips profit while letting losses run to 30 pips.
**Impact**: This inverts the profit equation - losing more when wrong.
**Fix**: Define exit targets BEFORE entering. Not negotiable.

### Mistake #5: Emotional Trading After Losses
**Error**: Increasing position size after a loss trying to "make it back quickly."
**Impact**: Losses compound. A 5% loss becomes 10-15% due to larger revenge trades.
**Fix**: Follow your position sizing rules religiously, especially after losses. The best traders trade smaller after losses, not larger.

A: Scanning 15-30 minutes per session is usually sufficient if using alerts. Position trades require less hands-on time than scalping. You do NOT need to watch charts all day for success.

**Q8: What if I enter a trade and immediately move into profit, then reverses?**

A: Lock in partial profits at first target even if you're only slightly profitable. Trail the remainder. This provides peace of mind and removes emotional pressure on the remaining position.

**Q9: How do I prevent over-trading with this strategy?**

A: Set a maximum daily trade limit (e.g., 3-5 trades max per day). Quality > Quantity. One well-executed trade beats five marginal trades. Only enter setups meeting all criteria strictly.
